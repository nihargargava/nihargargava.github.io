Hecke Characters

Contents

  • Conductor of a Hecke character

Let \(K\) be a number field. A Hecke Grossencharacter, or simply known as Hecke character, is a continuous complex-valued multiplicative homomorphism from the idele class group of \(K\) to \(\mathbb{C}^{\times}\).

The idele class group is defined as \[\begin{equation} \mathop{\mathrm{\mathfrak{C}}}(K) = \mathbb{A}_K^{\times} / K^{\times}. \end{equation}\]

Conductor of a Hecke character

Let \(M_K\) be the set of all places of \(K\) and let \(\mathfrak{m}\) be a \(K\)-modulus. This means that \(\mathfrak{m} :M_K \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\) is a finitely supported sequence such that for any real place \(v \in M_K\) one has \(\mathfrak{m}_v \in \{0,1\}\) and for complex places \(w\), we have \(\mathfrak{m}_w = 0\).
