Dirichlet’s Unit Theorem

Recall \(\mathcal{O}_{K} \subseteq K_{\mathbb{R}}\) with respect to the Minkowski’s embedding.

Let \(K_{\mathbb{R}}^{*} \subseteq K_{\mathbb{R}}\) be the set \(\{ x \in K_{\mathbb{R}} \ | \ \mathop{\mathrm{N}}(x) \neq 0 \}\). From this, we can define the following logarithm mapping \[\begin{align} \mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{log}}}: K_{\mathbb{R}}^{*} \rightarrow & \ \ \ \mathbb{R}^{r_1 + r_2}\\ x \mapsto & \ \ \ (\log | \sigma(x)|)_{\sigma}. \end{align}\]

This is a homomorphism of abelian groups. We know that \(\mathcal{O}_{K}^{*} \subset K_{\mathbb{R}}^{*}\) maps under this to a discrete subgroup of rank \(r_1+r_2-1\) and has a kernel of exactly the torsional units. The image of the \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{log}}}\) embedding is the vector subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^{r_1+r_2}\) perpendicular to \((1,1,1,\cdots,1)\).

The group \(\mathcal{O}_{K}^{*}\) has finite covolume inside the abelian group \(K_{\mathbb{R}}^{(1)} = \{ x \in K_{\mathbb{R}} \ | \ |\mathop{\mathrm{N}}(x)|=1\}\).

The covolume of \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{log}}}(\mathcal{O}_{K}^{*}) \subseteq (1,1,1,\cdots,1)^{\perp}\) is called the regulator of \(K\). It is denoted by \(R_K\).
