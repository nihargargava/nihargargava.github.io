Selberg Trace Formula

Contents

  • How to prove

Let \(f:\mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{C}\) be a function that is integrable, has a holomorphic Fourier transform in a strip \[\begin{equation} \{z \in \mathbb{C} \mid |\Im(z)| < \frac{1}{2} + \varepsilon\} \end{equation}\] for some \(\varepsilon > 0\) and satisfies that \[\begin{equation} |\hat{f}(z)| = O\left( \frac{1}{1+ |z|^{p}}\right) . \end{equation}\]

Let \(M\) be a closed hyperbolic surface of curvature \(-1\) and genus \(g\). Let \(0 = \lambda_0 \leq \lambda_1 \leq \lambda_2 \leq \dots\) be the eigenvalues of the Laplacian on \(M\) and let \(\mathcal{C}(M)\) be the set of closed oriented geodesics in \(M\). For any \(\gamma \in \mathcal{C}(M)\) we denote \(l(\gamma)\) to be the length and \(l(\gamma^{*})\) to be the length of the shortest geodesic \(\gamma^{*}\) such that \(\gamma = (\gamma^{*})^{k}\) for some \(k \geq 1\) (so \(\gamma^{*}\) is \(\gamma\) without repititions).

Then, with these assumptions, we can write the Selberg trace formula \[\begin{equation} \sum_{j=0}^{\infty}\hat{f}( 2 \pi \sqrt{ \lambda_{j}- \tfrac{1}{4} }) = 2(g-1)\int_{0}^{\infty}\hat{f}( 2 \pi x)x \tanh(\pi x) dx + \sum_{\gamma \in \mathcal{C}(M)} \frac{l(\gamma^{*}) f(l(\gamma))}{ 2 \sinh \left( l(\gamma)/2\right)} \end{equation}\]
