Gram-Schmidt Orthonormalization Process

This is a very generalized form of Gram-Schmidt algorithm. The setup for this is the same as that of real semisimple algebras with a positive involution, but in the special case when the semisimple algebra arises from a division \(\mathbb{Q}\)-algebra.

Let \(D\) be a division algebra over \(\mathbb{Q}\) and let \(D_{\mathbb{R}} = D \otimes_{\mathbb{Q}}\mathbb{R}\) and let \((\ )^{*}: D_{\mathbb{R}}\rightarrow D_{\mathbb{R}}\) be a positive involution.

Let \(k \ge 1\). Let us define \[\begin{align} \langle \ , \ \rangle_{D_{\mathbb{R}}} : D_{\mathbb{R}}^{k} \times D_{\mathbb{R}}^{k} & \mapsto D_{\mathbb{R}} \\ \langle x,y\rangle & = \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_{i}^{*} y_{i}. \end{align}\]

This form is not necessarily \(D_{\mathbb{R}}\)-bilinear, since \(D_{\mathbb{R}}\) may not be commutative. We can then define a real bilinear form on \(D_{\mathbb{R}}^{k}\) given by \[\begin{align} \langle x,y\rangle_{\mathbb{R}} = \mathop{\mathrm{tr}}\left(\langle x, y\rangle_{D_{\mathbb{R}}}\right). \end{align}\]

Note that, for any \(x \in D_{\mathbb{R}}^{k} \setminus \{ 0\}\), we have that \(\langle x,x\rangle_{D_{\mathbb{R}}}\) is a symmetric positive definite element of \(D_{\mathbb{R}}\) as defined in here. Hence, in particular \(\langle x,x\rangle_{D_{\mathbb{R}}}\) is a zero non-divisor in \(D_{\mathbb{R}}\) and therefore is a unit. Therefore, it makes sense to talk about \(\langle x,x\rangle_{D_{\mathbb{R}}}^{-1} \in D_{\mathbb{R}}\).
