Recollection

Let \(K = \{ x \in \mathbb{R}^{n} \mid g_{1}(x) \geq 0 , \dots, g_{m}(x) \geq 0\}\) be compact for some \(g_{i} \in \mathbb{R}[x] \in \mathbb{R}[x_{1},\dots,x_{n]}\).

Want to minimize some polynomial \(p(x)\). This is stated as (POP) \({P}^{\min} = \inf\{ p(x) \mid x \in K\} = \sup \{ \lambda \in \mathbb{R} \mid p - \lambda \in P(K)\}\), Here \[\begin{equation} P(K) = \{p \in \mathbb{R}[X] \mid p \geq 0\}, \end{equation}\] \[\begin{equation} \Sigma = \{ \Sigma q_{i}(x)^{2} \mid q_{i} \in \mathbb{R}[X] \}, \end{equation}\]

We call relaxation \(P_{d}^{\SOS} = \sup \{ \lambda \in \mathbb{R} \mid p - \lambda \in Q(g_{1}\dots,g_{n})\} \geq d\) where \[\begin{equation} Q(g_{1},\dots,g_{n}) = \{ \sigma_{0} + g_{1} \sigma_{1} +\dots + g_{m} \sigma_{m} \mid \sigma_{i} \in \Sigma \} . \end{equation}\]

One has \(P_{d}^{\SOS} \rightarrow P^{\SOS} = P^{\mind}\)

Duality

\[\begin{align} P^{\min} & = \inf \{ \int p(x) \diff \mu (x) \mid \mu \text{ prob measure supported on $K$} \} \\ & \leq P^{\min} \leq p(x) \end{align}\]

\[\begin{equation} P^{\min} = \inf \{ L(p) \mid L \in \mathbb{R}[x]^{*}, \text{ integration wrt prob. measure supported on }K\} \end{equation}\]

(Havilland, 1936)

For \(L \in \mathbb{R}[X]^{*}\) and \(K \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n}\) closed, the following are equivalent

  1. \(L\) is an integration with respect to a measure supported on \(K\)
  2. \(L(p) \geq 0\) \(\forall p \in {P}(K)\).
