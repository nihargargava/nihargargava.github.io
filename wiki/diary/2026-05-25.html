Effective equidistribution & unipotent flows of representations of integral quadratic forms

The speaker is Elon Lindenstrauss. This is joint work with Einsiedler, Mohammadi, Wieser (in the audience)

Starting point is the following theorem.

\(\text{\bf Ratner}\)

Let \(G\) be a real group, \(\Gamma \subseteq G\) be a latice. Let \(u_{t}\) be a \(1\)-parameter \(\mathrm{Ad}\)-unipotent group. Then for any \(f \in \mathcal{C}_{b}(G/\Gamma)\), \(\forall x \in G/\Gamma\), \[\begin{equation} \frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{T} f(u_{t},x) \mathrm{d}t \xrightarrow[]{} \int_{L \cdot x}^{} f . \end{equation}\] Here \(L \cdot x\) is a preiodic orbit for some \(\{u_{t}\} \subseteq L \subseteq G\).

One has that \(L \cdot x\) is periodic if and only if it supports an \(L\) invariant probbaility measure, which is if and only if \(\mathrm{stab}_{L}(x)\) is a lattice in L.

The proof of Ratner’s theorem is via measure classification.

\(\text{\bf Ratner}\)

Let \(G\) be a real group, \(\Gamma \subseteq G\) be a latice. Let \(u_{t}\) be a \(1\)-parameter \(\mathrm{Ad}\)-unipotent group as before. Let \(\mu\) be \(u_{t}\)-invariant and ergodic measure on \(G/\Gamma\), then \(\mu\) is homogeneous. This means that \(\mathrm{stab}_{G} \mu\) acts transitively on \(\mathrm{supp}\mu\), which happens if and only if \(\mu\) is the natural homogeneous measure on a periodic orbit of some \(\{u_{t}\} \subseteq L \subseteq G\).

Theorem of Shah

Let \(G\) be a real algebraic group. Let \(\varphi: \mathrm{SL}_{2}(\mathbb{R}) \rightarrow G\) be homogeneous. Let and \[\begin{equation} \mu_{s} : \varphi \begin{bmatrix} 1 & s \\ & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix}, a_{t} = \varphi \begin{bmatrix} \exp(\tfrac{t}{2}) & \\ & \exp(-\tfrac{t}{2}) \\ \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation}\] These are \(1\)-parameter unipotent and diagonal groups such that \(a_{t} u_{s} a_{-t} = u_{e^{t}s}\).

\(\text{\bf Shah}\)

\[\begin{equation} \lim_{t \xrightarrow[]{} \infty} \int_{0}^{1} f(a_{t} u_{s} x) \mathrm{d}s \xrightarrow[]{} \int_{L \cdot x}^{} f , \end{equation}\] where \(\varphi(\mathrm{SL}_{2}(\mathbb{R})) \subseteq L \subseteq G\).

Here \[\begin{equation} \int_{0}^{1} f(a_{t} u_{s} x) \mathrm{d}s = \tfrac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{T} f(u_{s} a_{t} x) \mathrm{d}s, T = e^{t}. \end{equation}\]

Ratner type measures

This can be thought of as a random walk. One has \[\begin{equation} \mu_{t} = \int_{0}^{1} \delta_{a_{t} u_{s}} \mathrm{d}s, \end{equation}\] One has \[\begin{equation} \int_{}^{} f(g x) \mathrm{d}\mu_{t}^{* k}(g) \simeq \int_{0}^{1} f(a_{kt} u_{s} x) \mathrm{d}s + O(e^{-t} ) \|f\|_{\infty}. \end{equation}\]

We say that a homogeneous measure on \(G/\Gamma\) is of Ratner type if there is some \(1\)-parameter unipotent flow under which it is invariant and ergodic.

Example of homogeneous measures not of Ratner type is the following. There are periodic \(L \cdot x\) where \(L\) is a torus (in the sense of algebraic groups).

\(\text{ \bf (Mozez-Shah) }\)

If \(\mu_{i}\) are homogeneous measures of Ratner type, either \(\mathrm{supp}\mu_{i}\) escapes from every compact set or after passing to subsequence, \(\mu_{i} \rightarrow \mu\) such that \(\mu\) is homogeneous of Ratner’s type and \[\begin{equation} \exists g_{i} \rightarrow 1 \text{ such that } \mathrm{supp}( g_{i} \mu_{i}) \subseteq \mathrm{supp}( \mu) \end{equation}\]

Arithmetic group

The arithmetic case is the most interesting. Let \(\mathbf{G}\) be an algebraic group defined over \(\mathbb{Q}\) (or some number field \(F\)), let \(G = \mathbf{G}(\mathbb{R})/\Gamma\), Let \(\Gamma \subseteq \mathbf{G}(\mathbb{Q})\) be a congruence lattice (commensurable to \(\mathbf{G}(\mathbb{Z})\)). Let it be such that \(\mathbf{G}\) has no \(\mathbb{Q}\)-characters.

One can view it all together as an Adelic quotient if we consider \(\mathbf{G}(\mathbb{A})/\mathbf{G}(\mathbb{Q})\). Here \(\mathbb{A}\) is the restricted product \[\begin{equation} \mathbb{A} = \mathbb{R} \times \prod_{}^{}' \mathbb{Q}_{p} \end{equation}\]

One has \(\mathrm{SL}_{n}(\mathbb{A})/\mathrm{SL}_{n}(\mathbb{Q}) \rightarrow \mathrm{SL}_{n}(\mathbb{R}) / \mathrm{SL}_{n}(\mathbb{Z})\mathbb{Z}\) as a surjective map. Similar construction also works for number fields.

Classification of measures on Adelic quotients

Let \(\mathbf{G}(\mathbb{A}_{F})/\mathbf{G}(F)\) be an Adelic quotient for a number field \(F\). Two assumptions are needed.

  1. \(\mathbf{G}\) is semisimple
  2. \(\mathbf{G}\) is anisotropic $ $ \(\mathbf{G}(\mathbb{A}_{F})/\mathbf{G}(F)\) compact.

Under these assumptions, we give a concise description of all homogenous measures of Ratner’s type in \(\mathbf{G}(\mathbb{A}_{F})/\mathbf{G}(F)\). Let \(\mathbf{H}\) be a simply connected semisimple group over \(F\). Let \(\rho: \mathbf{H} \rightarrow \mathbf{G}\) be an \(F\)-homomorphism. Let \(g \in \mathbf{G}(\mathbb{A})\). Let \[\begin{equation} g[\rho(\mathbf{H}(\mathbb{A}))] \subseteq \mathbf{G}(\mathbb{A})/ \mathbf{G}(F), \end{equation}\] these are precisely all the homogeneous measures of Ratner’s type in \(\mathbf{G}(\mathbb{A}_{F})/\mathbf{G}(F)\) (parameterized by \((\mathbf{H},\rho,g)\)). These are called “Adelic periods”.

It is useful to think \(\mathbf{G}(\mathbb{A}_{F})/\mathbf{G}(F)\) as another Adelic period in \(\mathrm{SL}_{n}(\mathbb{A}_{F})/\mathrm{SL}_{n}(F)\). To an Adelic period can attach some notion of height/complexities. \[\begin{equation} \prod_{v}^{} \| \text{ something }\|_{v} = \tilde{H} ( \text{ adelic periods }) \end{equation}\]

\((\text{ \bf Elon et al})\)

Assuming adelic period correponds to \((\mathbf{H}, \rho, g)\) is contained in \((\mathbf{G},\tau,g_{0})\), one has the difference of the Adelic periods is \[\begin{equation} | \int_{}^{} f - \int_{}^{} f | \ll _{n,d} \frac{\tilde(H)( \mathbf{G},\tau,g)^{A}}{\min \tilde{H}(\mathbb{H}^{1}, \rho^{1},g^{1})^{\frac{1}{A}} } \disc(F)^{A}. \end{equation}\]

This is minimum over all Adelic periods strictly containing \((\mathbf{H},\rho,g)\) and strictly contained in \((\mathbf{G},\tau,g_{0})\). This generalizes E-M-M-V or E-M-V in case \(\mathbf{H} \subseteq \mathbf{G}\) is maximal.
