The seminar

Let \(L/\mathbb{Q}\) be a number field (we will assume \(\mathrm{Gal}(L/\mathbb{Q})\) is Abelian and the number field is Galois).

Let \(F \in \mathbb{Z}[s,t]\) be a homogenuous polynomial of degree \(2\) that is irreducible over \(\mathbb{Q}\). We will study \[\begin{equation} (*) \ \ \ \ \ \mathrm{N}_{L/\mathbb{Q}}(x) = F(s,t) , x \in L \end{equation}\] For \(B \geq 2\), define \[\begin{equation} \mathrm{N}_{F/L}(B)= \#\{ (s,t) \in [-B,B]^{2} \mid \exists x \in L \text{ such that }(*) \text{ holds for }s,t\}. \end{equation}\] We will let \(B \rightarrow \infty\)

The conjecture of Longhram-Smeet is the following. Let \[\begin{equation} \pi: X \rightarrow \mathbb{P}^{m} \end{equation}\] be a morphism that is dominant on generic fibres and geom. irreducible (what is that?) and let \(X\) be projective and smooth. Then \[\begin{equation} N_{\mathrm{glob}}(\pi,B) = \#\{ x \in \mathbb{P}^{n}(\mathbb{Q}) \mid H(x) \leq B, \pi^{-1}(x) \text{ has a rational point}\} \end{equation}\] This is bounded by \[\begin{equation} N_{\mathrm{loc}}(\pi,B) = \#\{ x \in \mathbb{P}^{n}(\mathbb{Q}) \mid H(x) \leq B, \pi^{-1}(x) \text{ is ELS}\} \end{equation}\]

ELS stands for everywhere locally solvable.

(2016)

\[\begin{equation} N_{\mathrm{loc}}(\pi,B) \ll \frac{B^{n+1}}{(\log B)^{\Delta(\pi)}} \text{ \ \ } \Delta(\pi) \geq 0 \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} N_{\mathrm{loc}}(\pi,B) \sim_{B \rightarrow \infty} c \frac{B^{n+1}}{(\log B)^{\Delta(\pi)}} \text{ for some } c> 0. \end{equation}\]

We expect that \[\begin{equation} N_{L,F}(B) \sim_{B \rightarrow +\infty} \frac{B^{2}}{ (\log B)^{\alpha_{L,F}}} \end{equation}\] where \[\begin{equation} \alpha_{L,F} = \begin{cases} 1 - \frac{1}{[L:\mathbb{Q}]} & \text{ if } F \text{ irred. on }L \\ 1 - \frac{2}{[L:\mathbb{Q}]} & \text{ otherwise} \end{cases} \end{equation}\]

For \(\pi: X \rightarrow \mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{Q}}^{2}\), and \(D = [L:\mathbb{Q}]\) we have \(X \rightarrow \mathrm{N}_{L/\mathbb{Q}}(x) = F(s,t) \subseteq \mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{Q}}^{D} \times \mathbb{A}_{Q}^{2}\),

For ex: \(L = \mathbb{Q}[X]/\langle X^{3} - 3 X - 1\rangle\)

(Da Silva)

We assume that \(\mathcal{O}_{L}\) is principal and that there is exists \((x_{0},s_{0},t_{0})\) such that \[\begin{equation} \mathrm{N}_{L/\mathbb{Q}}(x_{0}) = F(s_{0},t_{0}), \end{equation}\] then \[\begin{equation} \mathrm{N}_{F,L}(B) \asymp\frac{B^{2}}{ (\log B)^{\alpha_{L,F}}}. \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{align} N_{L,F}(B) & = \sum_{|s| , |t| \leq B} \mathbb{1}_{\exists x \in L: N_{L/\mathbb{Q}}(x) = F(s,t)} \\ & \geq \sum_{|s|,|t| \leq B}^{} \mu^{2}(F(s,t)) \prod_{p \mid F(s,t)}^{} \mathbb{1}_{\exists x \in L/\mathbb{Q}: \mathrm{N}(x)=p} & \geq \sum_{|s|,|t| \leq B}^{} \mu^{2}(F(s,t)) \prod_{p \mid F(s,t)}^{} \mathbb{1}_{\exists \mathcal{P} \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{L}: \mathrm{N}(\mathcal{P})=p} \end{align}\]

We let \[\begin{equation} G = \mathrm{Gal}(L/\mathbb{Q}), \widehat{G}= \{ 1 , \chi_{1},\dots,\chi_{p-1}\} \text{ for Dirichlet characters } \chi_{i} \text{ of conductor }q \end{equation}\] Denote \[\begin{equation} r_{L}(n) = \{ \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{L} \mid \mathrm{N}_{L/\mathbb{Q}}( \mathfrak{a} ) = n\} \end{equation}\] We have \(r_{L}(n) = ( 1 * \chi_{1} * \chi_{2} \dots * \chi_{p-1})(n)\). Then \[\begin{equation} N_{L,F}(B) \geq \sum_{_{|s|,|t| \in B}}^{} \mu^{2}(F(s,t)) \frac{r_{L}(F(s,t))}{ D^{\omega(F(s,t))}}. \end{equation}\] If \(D=2\), then \[\begin{equation} \frac{1}{D^{\omega(k)}} = \sum_{d \mid k} \mu(d) \left( 1- \frac{1}{D}\right)^{\omega(k)} \mu(d) \end{equation}\] We take \(\overline{\lambda_{D}}\) supported in \([1,B^{\varepsilon}]\) and estimate this by \[\begin{equation} N_{L,F}(B) \geq \sum_{d}^{} \overline{\lambda_{d}} \left( 1- \tfrac{1}{D}\right)^{w(d)} \underbrace{\sum_{\substack{|s|,|t| \leq B \\ d \mid F(s,t)}}^{} \mu^{2}(F(s,t) ) \Omega_{L}(F(s,t))}_{M_{d}(B)}, \end{equation}\] and then \[\begin{equation} M_{d}^{*}(B) = \sum_{\substack{|s|,|t| \leq B \\ d \mid F(s,t)} }^{} r_{L}(F(s,t)) \end{equation}\] which is \[\begin{equation} = \sum_{k \leq c B^{2}}^{} \psi_{L}(k) \#\{ (s,t) \in [- B,B]^{2} : [k,d] \mid F(s,t) \} \end{equation}\] Here \[\begin{equation} \psi_{L}(n) = \sum_{k_{1},\dots k_{D-1}} \chi_{1}(k_{1}) \dots \chi_{D-1}(k_{D-1}) \end{equation}\] and \[\begin{equation} \Omega_{L}(n) = (1 * \psi_L)(n) \end{equation}\]

Fix \(Z = B^{\frac{2}{D}}\). Contribution of \((k_{1},\dots,k_{D-1}) \in [1,2]^{D-1}\): if there exists \(k_{i} > Z\) then \(k_{i}',k_{1},\dots,k_{D-1} = F(s,t)\) and then \[\begin{equation} \chi_{1}(k_{1}) \chi_{2}(k_{2}) \dots \chi_{D-1}(k_{D-1}) = \underbrace{\chi_{1}(F(s,t))}_{=1 (\text{why?})} \chi_{1}^{-1}(k_{1}' k_{2} \dots k_{D-1}) \chi_{2}(k_{2}) \chi_{D-1}(k_{D-1}), \end{equation}\]

Denote \[\begin{equation} \Lambda_{k,d}(B) = \#\{ (s,t) \in [- B,B]^{2} : [k,d] \mid F(s,t) \}, \end{equation}\] Then \[\begin{align} \sum_{\substack{|s|,|t| \leq B \\ d \mid F(s,t)}}^{} (1 * \chi * \chi^{2}) ( F(s,t) ) & = \sum_{k_{1},k_{2} \leq B}^{} \chi(k_{1}) \chi^{2}(k_{1}) \Lambda_{k,d}(B) \\ & = \sum_{ (k_{1},k_{2}) \in [1,B^{\frac{2}{3}}]}^{} \chi(k_{1}) \chi^{2}(k_{2}) \lambda_{k,d}(B) + \sum_{k_{1} > B^{\frac{2}{3}}}^{} \chi(k_{1}) \chi^{2}(k_{2}) \Lambda_{k,d}(B) \dots \end{align}\]

\[\begin{equation} k_{1}' = \frac{F(s,t)}{k_{1} k_{2}} \end{equation}\] \[\begin{equation} \sum_{k'_1, k_{2} \in[1,B^{\frac{2}{3}}]^{2}}^{} \chi( \frac{F(s,t)}{ k_{1} k_{2}}) \chi^{2}(k_{2}) \Lambda_{d,k}(B) = \sum_{k'_1 k_{2} \in [1,B^{\frac{2}{3}}]}^{} \chi^{2}(k_{1}) \chi (k_{2}) {\Lambda_{d,k}(B)} \end{equation}\]

With all this gymnastics that I tried to faithfully copy, one can conclude that \[\begin{equation} M_{d}(B) = g(d) B^{2} + O(B^{2- \varepsilon}), \text{ where }g(d) \simeq \frac{1}{d} \end{equation}\]
