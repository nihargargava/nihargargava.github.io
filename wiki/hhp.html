Harmonic Homogeneous Polynomials

Contents

  • Theta functions

\(\DeclareMathOperator{\covol}{covol}\)

Consider the Laplacian operator \(\Delta\) on \(R^{n}\). We say that a polynomial \(P \in \mathbb{C}[x_{1},\dots,x_{n}]\) is homogeneous and harmonic if it is homogeneous and \[\begin{equation} \Delta (P) = 0. \end{equation}\]

Theta functions

Let \(\Lambda \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n}\) be any lattice. For a harmonic homogenous polynomial \(P:\mathbb{R}^{n} \rightarrow \mathbb{C}\), with \(\deg P = d\), one defines \[\begin{equation} \theta_{\Lambda,P}(\tau) = \sum_{v \in \Lambda}^{} P(v) e^{ i \pi \|v\|^{2} \tau}. \end{equation}\]

The functional equation of the theta function is \[\begin{equation} \theta_{\Lambda,P}(\tau) = \covol(\Lambda) i^{d} \tau^{-\tfrac{n}{2}-d} \theta_{\hat{\Lambda},P}(\tfrac{1}{\tau}), \end{equation}\] where \(\hat{\Lambda}\) is the dual of \(\Lambda\).
