The classical case

An Eisenstein series over \(\Gamma = SL_2(\mathbb{Z})\) is the following series \[\begin{equation} E(s,z) = \sum_{ \gamma \in \Gamma_{\infty} \backslash \Gamma } |\Im ( \gamma z ) |^{s}, \end{equation}\] where \(\Gamma_\infty\) is the group of matrices that fix \(\infty\) on the upper half-plane,
namely \(\pm \left( \begin{smallmatrix} 1 & \mathbb{Z} \\ 0 & 1 \end{smallmatrix} \right)\).

This series is absolutely convergent for \(\Re(s)> 1\) (but meromorphically continues in the \(s\) variable to \(\mathbb{C}\)). On the other hand, in the \(s\) variable, it is automorphic under the action of \(\Gamma\) and is an eigenfunction of the Laplacian on \(\mathbb{H}\).
