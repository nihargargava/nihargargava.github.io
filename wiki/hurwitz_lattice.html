Hurwitz Integer Lattice

Consider \(\mathbb{H}\) to be the Hamiltonian quaternions. We define the subring \(H \subseteq \mathbb{H}\) as \[\begin{equation} H = \{ a + b i + cj +dk \mid (a,b,c,d) \in \mathbb{Z}^{4} \sqcup (\tfrac{1}{2} + \mathbb{Z})^{4}\}. \end{equation}\]

\(H\) contains \(L = \mathbb{Z} +\mathbb{Z}i + \mathbb{Z} j + \mathbb{Z} k\) as an index \(2\) sublattice within itself. To see this, we notice that \(H/L \rightarrow \mathbb{F}_2^{4}\) is a natural injective map such that the image only contains \((1,1,1,1)\).

One can endow \(\mathbb{H}\) with the norm given by \[\begin{equation} a + b i + cj + d k \mapsto a^{2} + b^{2} + c^{2} + d^{2}. \end{equation}\] This norm is multiplicative and is a quadratic form on \(\mathbb{H}\). Under the Euclidean norm induced by this, \(H\) has 24 minimal vectors, each of length \(1\). They are \[\begin{equation} \pm 1 , \pm i , \pm j , \pm k , \tfrac{1}{2}(\pm 1 \pm i \pm j \pm k). \end{equation}\] This lattice is also called the D4 lattice. See the page here.

The packing density is then \[\begin{equation} \frac{1}{16} \cdot {2} \cdot \frac{\pi^{2}}{2!} = \frac{\pi^2}{16}. \end{equation}\]
