Three point bound for sphere packing

Caution: Unpublished work of Cohn, Salmon, de Laat

Let \(\Delta_{3,n}\) be the infimum of \(\mathop{\mathrm{vol}}(B_{1/2})(f_2(0)+f_2(0))\) over pairs of functions \(f_{2}:\mathbb{R}^{n} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) and \(f_{3}: \mathbb{R}^{n} \times \mathbb{R}^{n} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) such that

  • \(f_2\) is radial
  • \(\hat{f}_{2} \ge 0\) and \(\hat{f}_{2}(0)=1\),
  • \(f_{3}(x,y)=f_{3}(gx,gy)\), for \(g \in O(n)\).
  • \(f_{3}\) is positive definite as a kernel, i.e.Â \(\sum_{x,y}^{}w_{x} w_{y} \ge 0\),
  • \(f_{2}(x)+f_{3}(x,0)+f_{3}(0,x)+f_{3}(x,x) \le 0\) for \(\|x\| \ge 1\).
  • \(f_{3}(x,y) + f_{3}(x,x-y)+f_{3}(y,y-x) \le 0\) for \(\|x\|,\|y\|,\|x-y\| \ge 1\).

Then, \(\Delta_{3,n}\) is a sphere packing bound in \(n\) dimensions.
