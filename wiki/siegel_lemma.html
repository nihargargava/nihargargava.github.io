Siegel’s lemma

It is an existence result due to Siegel. It says that given \(N > M\) and an \(M \times N\) non-zero matrix \(A\) with entries in \(\mathbb{Z}\), then the equation \[\begin{equation} Ax = 0, \end{equation}\] where \(x \in \mathbb{Z}^{N}\) has a solution with all the entries bounded above by \((NB)^{\frac{M}{N-M}}\) where \(B\) is an upper bound for the matrix entries of \(A\).

Bombieri and Vaaler improved the upper bound to \[\begin{equation} |x|_{\infty} \le \left( D^{-1}\sqrt{ \det(A A^{T})}\right)^{\frac{1}{N-M}}, \end{equation}\] where \(D\) is the gcd of the \(M \times M\) minors of the matrix \(A\). This last term is actually the Arakelov height of the rational subspace generated by \(A\).
