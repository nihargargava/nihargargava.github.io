Fourier transform

On \(\mathbb{R}^d\), we define the following map to be the Fourier transform.

\[\begin{align} \mathcal{F}: \mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R}^{d}) \mapsto & \ \mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R}^{d}) \\ f \mapsto & \mathcal{F}(f) = \hat{f} \\ & \hat{f}(\xi) = \int_{\mathbb{R}^{d}} f(x)e^{-2\pi i \langle x,\xi \rangle} dx \end{align}\]

Let \(g_{z}=e^{2 \pi i z \| x \|^{2}}\). Then \(\mathcal{F}(g_{z})= \left( \frac{i}{z}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} g_{-\frac{1}{z}}\). This is example of a Gaussian.
